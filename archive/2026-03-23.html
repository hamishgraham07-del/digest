The smart play today is patience. When the VIX spikes 11% in a single session, that's fear, not fundamentals. The underlying US economy and corporate earnings haven't changed — it's geopolitical risk premium being repriced. Watch for the Strait of Hormuz deadline (Wednesday) as the next catalyst. If diplomacy prevails, this sell-off becomes a buying opportunity. If it escalates, energy stocks run further.

🌍 World News

Iran War Day 23: Trump's 48-Hour Hormuz Ultimatum Rattles Global Markets

The US-Israeli military campaign against Iran has entered its 23rd day with no signs of de-escalation. President Trump issued an ultimatum on Sunday demanding Iran fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz within 48 hours or face strikes on Iranian power plants. The Strait — through which roughly 20% of global oil and a third of the world's fertiliser shipments pass — has been effectively closed since early March. Israeli airstrikes hit eastern Tehran overnight, while Iranian retaliatory missile strikes injured nearly 100 people near an Israeli nuclear facility. This is the single biggest driver of market volatility right now, and the Wednesday deadline is the next major catalyst.

💡 This directly explains why every market is red today except oil and energy stocks. For your watchlist, WDS and STO benefit from elevated oil prices, while NDQ and broad indices face headwinds until the geopolitical risk premium unwinds.

Cuba's Power Grid Collapses for Third Time in March

Cuba plunged into a nationwide blackout on Sunday — the third complete power grid failure this month. The country's ageing electricity infrastructure, which was already struggling under chronic underinvestment, has been pushed to breaking point by a US-imposed oil blockade that has choked off fuel supplies to thermal power stations. Millions of Cubans are without electricity, running water, and air conditioning in tropical heat. International aid has been slow, and the government has been unable to stabilise the grid for more than a few days at a stretch.

💡 This is a downstream consequence of the broader US-Iran confrontation disrupting global oil supply chains. It's a reminder that energy security is the defining geopolitical issue of 2026, which is why energy stocks are outperforming.

Sudan Hospital Strike Kills 64 Including 13 Children in Darfur

At least 64 people, including 13 children, were killed in a strike on a hospital in Sudan's western Darfur region, according to the World Health Organization. The attack is the latest atrocity in Sudan's ongoing civil war, which has created one of the world's worst humanitarian crises with millions displaced. The WHO condemned the strike as a violation of international humanitarian law, but international attention remains largely focused on the Middle East conflict, leaving Sudan's crisis chronically under-resourced and under-reported.

💡 While not directly market-moving, Sudan's crisis compounds global food insecurity alongside the Hormuz shipping disruption. Fertiliser supply chains through the Strait of Hormuz affect food production globally, and Sudan was already facing famine before this escalation.

Brazil's Lula Slams US for 'Thinking They Own the World'

Brazilian President Lula da Silva publicly criticised the United States for its military campaign against Iran, saying Washington acts as if it owns the world. The remarks reflect growing frustration among BRICS nations and the Global South with US foreign policy, and come as Brazil prepares to host the G20 later this year. Lula's comments were echoed by several Latin American leaders and add to the diplomatic isolation the US faces over its Middle East strategy outside of its traditional Western allies.

💡 This signals growing geopolitical fragmentation that could accelerate de-dollarisation trends. For markets, it means the US may face more resistance to unilateral action, which could prolong the Hormuz crisis rather than resolve it quickly.

🇦🇺 Australia

RBA Hikes Again to 4.10% — Banks Tipping 4.35% by May

The Reserve Bank of Australia has raised the cash rate by 25 basis points to 4.10%, marking the second consecutive rate hike in 2026 after last year's brief easing cycle. The Board voted 5-4, signalling this was a close call but that persistent inflation — particularly in housing (+6.8%) and food (+3.1%) — left them no choice. ANZ and NAB have both revised their forecasts to include another 25bp hike in May to 4.35%, which would take rates back to their 2024 peak. CPI is running at 3.8% annually, still well above the RBA's 2-3% target band, and the Board flagged 'rising capacity pressures' as a key concern.

💡 Higher rates mean your cash position earns more in savings/term deposits while you wait for better market entry points. For housing, this puts further pressure on mortgage holders and could cool property prices — relevant if you're watching the market. For the ASX, rate-sensitive sectors like REITs and banks will feel the pinch.

GDP Growth Accelerates to 2.6% — Strongest in Over a Year

Australia's economy grew 0.8% in the December quarter, pushing annual growth to 2.6% — the strongest pace in more than a year. The result exceeded expectations and was driven by government spending, a rebound in household consumption, and strong exports (partly thanks to elevated commodity prices). However, per-capita GDP remains weak, meaning the average Australian isn't feeling the growth. The strong headline number actually gave the RBA more room to hike rates, as it suggests the economy can handle tighter monetary policy without tipping into recession.

💡 Strong GDP + rising rates = the RBA has cover to keep tightening. This is a double-edged sword: the economy isn't crashing (good for ASX earnings), but rates will stay higher for longer (bad for valuations and housing affordability).

Sydney and Melbourne Listings Surge as Sellers Test the Market

New property listings in Sydney and Melbourne have picked up sharply through February and into March, with sellers looking to capitalise before further rate hikes potentially cool buyer demand. However, the picture is uneven: Perth listings remain 48% below the five-year average, Brisbane 31% below, and Adelaide 23% below — creating a two-speed market where the eastern capitals are loosening while the resource states stay tight. Property analytics firm Cotality noted that the March rate hike is likely to cool demand further but could intensify competition at the lower end of the market as first-home buyers race to lock in before rates potentially hit 4.35%.

💡 If you're watching the housing market, more listings in Sydney means more choice and potentially softer prices — especially if rates go to 4.35% in May. The resource state tightness (Perth, Brisbane) reflects the energy/mining boom that's also lifting WDS and STO.

🤖 AI & Technology

Trump Signs AI Accountability Act — Mandatory Bias Audits for Business

The US has passed the AI Accountability Act, the first major federal AI legislation, requiring companies deploying AI in consequential decisions (hiring, lending, insurance, healthcare) to conduct and publish regular bias audits. The law establishes a new AI oversight body within the FTC and imposes fines up to $10M per violation for non-compliance. This is a significant shift from the previous 'voluntary commitments' approach and brings the US closer to EU-style AI regulation. For AI automation firms, this creates both risk (compliance costs) and opportunity (demand for audit tools and governance platforms).

💡 Directly relevant to Purpletag. This creates a new service category: helping SMBs ensure their AI automations comply with bias audit requirements. If you're building AI workflows for clients, 'compliance-ready' becomes a selling point. Consider adding AI governance consulting to your service offerings.

Apple's Gemini-Powered Siri Overhaul Targets March Release with iOS 26.4

Apple is reportedly finalising a complete reimagining of Siri powered by Google's Gemini AI model, set to launch with iOS 26.4 this month. The new Siri will feature on-screen awareness (understanding what you're looking at on your phone), cross-app integration (performing multi-step tasks across different apps), and will run partly on-device via Apple's Private Cloud Compute infrastructure for privacy. This represents Apple's biggest AI play since launching Apple Intelligence last year, and signals that the AI assistant wars are heating up significantly.

💡 Apple going all-in on AI assistants validates the market for AI automation services. When Apple normalises AI-powered workflows for 2 billion iPhone users, the SMBs you work with will increasingly expect — and demand — similar AI automation in their business operations. Rising tide lifts all boats.

OpenAI's GPT-5.4 Hits 1M Token Context — Revenue Surpasses $25B Annualised

OpenAI unveiled GPT-5.4 with a 1-million-token context window and the ability to autonomously execute multi-step workflows, scoring 75% on the OSWorld-V benchmark (which simulates real desktop productivity tasks). The company has also surpassed $25 billion in annualised revenue and is reportedly taking early steps toward a public listing. Meanwhile, McKinsey's latest report found that 12% of current job tasks across the economy have been automated by AI over the past two years, but 8% of entirely new job categories were created in the same period — suggesting AI is reshaping rather than simply replacing the workforce.

💡 The 1M token context window is game-changing for the kind of document processing and workflow automation Purpletag builds. You can now feed entire business databases, years of emails, or complete project histories into a single prompt. This massively expands what's possible for SMB automation.
